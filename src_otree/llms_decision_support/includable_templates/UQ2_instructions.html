Setting and Task

The experiment itself consists of 2 different parts.

Part 1 revolves around a coffee network with 3 suppliers, 2 roasteries, and 3 customers. Suppliers provide raw coffee, roasteries roast it (light or dark roast), and the roasted coffee is shipped and sold to customers.

For part 1, you are a supply chain planner tasked with deciding which suppliers and roasteries to activate in this network. Your outcome for part 1 depends on the profit you achieve with your decisions.

{% include "llms_decision_support/part1_problem_setting.svg" %}

In part 2 of the experiment, you need to choose from a list of alternative payoff schemes. Your outcome for part 2 depends on this choice and an element of chance.

In the end of the experiment, either your outcome from part 1 or part 2 will randomly determine your payoff (50:50 chance).

If not stated otherwise, monetary values are given in the fictional currency Coffee Dollars (COF$). The conversion from Coffee Dollars (COF$) to {{ CURRENCY }} is
