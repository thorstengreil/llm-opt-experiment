Part 1: Decisions, Profit, Revenue & Costs

You will make activation decisions for suppliers and roasteries:

  • For each supplier, you choose whether to activate it or not
  • For each roastery, you decide whether to activate it in 'Low' or 'High' capacity mode, or to not activate it
You need to activate at least one supplier and one roastery to be able to deliver any coffee.

{% include "llms_decision_support/part1_problem_setting.svg" %}

Based on the decisions you make, you will generate profit. Profit is defined as revenue minus all costs:

  • Revenue comes from coffee sold to customers. Not all demand must be met
  • Fixed cost of activating any supplier apply only if you choose to activate this supplier
  • Fixed cost of activating any roastery apply solely if you decide to activate this roastery (capacity modes 'High' and 'Low' incur different cost)
  • Variable shipping cost apply to and from roasteries
  • Variable roasting cost apply per unit at roasteries (different for light and dark roasts)

The fictional company you work for has a performance bonus pool filled with COF$ {{ ENDOWMENT }}. The profit you receive from your decisions will be combined with this bonus pool, either increasing or decreasing its final size. This final size of the pool will determine your outcome for part 1 of the experiment.
